2. The "Stitch" Mechanism

The core axiom of the theory is that every particle is a helical excitation. It travels at a constant total speed of c (speed of light). This speed is split between moving through space (Drift) and rotating internally (Time/Mass).

3. Deriving Relativity

Because the total speed is fixed at c, if a particle moves faster through space, it must rotate slower internally. This simple geometric trade-off perfectly reproduces the Lorentz Time Dilation factor ($\gamma$) found in Einstein's Special Relativity.

Time Dilation

As velocity approaches c, the internal rotation frequency drops to zero. Time stops for a photon.

Mass Generation

Mass is identified as the rotational energy. Objects at rest have maximal rotation (rest mass). Photons have no rotation (zero mass).
